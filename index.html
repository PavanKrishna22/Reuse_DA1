SDG3: Good Health and Well-being

Transforming India's Healthcare Landscape

Understanding SDG3

Sustainable Development Goal 3 (SDG3) is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by the United Nations in 2015. The goal aims to "ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages" by 2030.

For India, with its population of over 1.4 billion people, SDG3 represents a crucial framework for addressing healthcare challenges and improving the overall well-being of its citizens. The goal encompasses various targets including:

  • Reducing maternal mortality
  • Ending preventable deaths of newborns and children
  • Combating communicable diseases
  • Ensuring universal access to healthcare services
  • Supporting research and development of vaccines and medicines

By 2030, India aims to achieve universal health coverage, including financial risk protection, access to quality essential healthcare services, and access to safe, effective, quality, and affordable essential medicines and vaccines for all.

Major Healthcare Initiatives

Ayushman Bharat

Launched in September 2018, Ayushman Bharat is the world's largest health insurance scheme, providing a cover of ₹5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. The program aims to reduce catastrophic healthcare expenditure for vulnerable families.

Key Achievements (2018-2024):

  • • Over 500 million citizens covered under the scheme
  • • More than 50 million beneficiaries treated
  • • 25,000+ hospitals empaneled across India
  • • ₹70,000+ crore worth of treatments provided
  • • 33 states and UTs implementing the scheme
  • • 1,900+ procedures covered under the scheme
  • • Reduction in out-of-pocket healthcare expenses by 30%
  • • Implementation of e-cards for seamless hospital services

Mission Indradhanush

Launched in December 2014, Mission Indradhanush aims to immunize all children under the age of 2 years and pregnant women against preventable diseases. The program was expanded as "Intensified Mission Indradhanush" in 2017 to reach more children in hard-to-reach areas.

Impact and Progress (2014-2024):

  • • 3.39 crore children immunized
  • • 87.18 lakh pregnant women covered
  • • Immunization coverage increased from 62% to 76%
  • • 12 vaccines now included in the program
  • • 680+ districts covered across India
  • • Special focus on 172 high-priority districts
  • • Reduction in childhood disease incidence by 18%
  • • Digital immunization tracking implemented nationwide

POSHAN Abhiyaan

Launched in March 2018, POSHAN Abhiyaan (National Nutrition Mission) is India's flagship program to improve nutritional outcomes for children, adolescent girls, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. It aims to reduce stunting, undernutrition, anemia, and low birth weight babies through convergence, use of technology and community engagement.

Achievements (2018-2024):

  • • 10+ crore children benefited
  • • Stunting reduction from 38.4% to 34.7%
  • • 14 lakh+ Anganwadi centers modernized
  • • 1.2 crore pregnant women supported annually
  • • Digital nutrition monitoring system implemented
  • • 85% Anganwadi workers trained in smartphone-based tracking
  • • 15% reduction in anemia among young children
  • • Community-Based Events (CBE) held monthly in 14 lakh AWCs

National Health Mission

Launched in 2013, combining the rural and urban health missions, NHM is India's largest public health program aimed at strengthening healthcare systems. It focuses on universal access to equitable, affordable, and quality healthcare services accountable and responsive to people's needs.

Key Impacts (2013-2024):

  • • IMR reduced from 50 to 28.6 per 1,000 births
  • • 1.5 lakh+ healthcare facilities upgraded
  • • 10 lakh+ ASHAs deployed nationwide
  • • 3,000+ mobile medical units operational
  • • 32,000+ ambulances under 108/102 services
  • • 80% increase in institutional deliveries
  • • 60% reduction in maternal mortality ratio since inception
  • • Implementation of Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) for early childhood screening
